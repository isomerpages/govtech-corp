<h3>What‚Äôs happening under the MOC?</h3>
<p>The three-year agreement will focus on the exchange of knowledge and experience
in areas including digital identity, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity,
cloud services and even digital technologies to tackle the COVID-19 pandemic.</p>
<p>On the National Digital Identity (NDI) front, for example, both countries
will explore the mutual recognition of verified credentials for their residents‚Äô
digital identities. This will benefit digital trade and people-to-people
flows between both countries.</p>
<p>GovTech and Japan‚Äôs Digital Agency will also work on building mutual capabilities
by sharing of skills and co-organising training courses, programmes and
workshops ‚Äì and supporting each other‚Äôs digital government events.</p>
<p>We can also look forward to meetings that foster collaboration between
the senior leaders of each agency.</p>
<p>Finally, the MOC will also see both agencies jointly publishing a Singapore-Japan
digital government report.</p>
<p>The MOC will be driven by these key principles:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>User-centric design in designing digital government services to serve
the needs of citizens and businesses</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Supporting open, inclusive and transparent government, including promoting
open standards and open-source technology</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Supporting accessible services for all</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>As Singapore and Japan celebrate 56 years of diplomatic ties, the new
