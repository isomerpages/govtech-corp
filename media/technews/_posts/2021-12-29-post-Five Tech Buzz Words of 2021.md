<h3>5G network</h3>
<p>The promise of super-fast mobile internet in the form of 5G has been around
for some time, but this year was when it got closer to reality here in
Singapore.</p>
<p>Consumers may be getting as many as&nbsp;<a href="https://www.businesstimes.com.sg/technology/singtel-m1-starhub-tie-up-and-tpg-provisionally-awarded-21-ghz-spectrum-rights-for-5g" class="editor-rtfLink" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">three standalone 5G networks</a>&nbsp;to
choose from, thanks to an awarding of network spectrum rights to Singtel,
an M1-Starhub consortium, and TPG.</p>
<p>They are aiming to deploy 5G with 50 per cent coverage within 2 years
and nationwide coverage within five years.</p>
<p>Meanwhile,&nbsp;<a href="https://www.tech.gov.sg/media/media-releases/2021-10-20-first-batch-of-public-sector-5g-trials-at-sentosa-underway" class="editor-rtfLink" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Sentosa has been serving as a testbed</a>&nbsp;for
trials of 5G‚Äôs application in public sector projects. The Building and
Construction Authority and National Environment Agency were among the first

<p>Singapore has been pushing to upgrade its infrastructure to support the

<p>Starting September 2021, more than 600 EV chargers will be deployed at
