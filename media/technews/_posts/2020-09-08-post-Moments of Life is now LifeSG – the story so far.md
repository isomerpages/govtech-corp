### **LifeSG was first conceived for newborns**

Every app has an origin story, and LifeSG‚Äôs started somewhat poetically for people just starting their life ‚Äì newborn babies and their parents.

### **Having one consolidated app wasn‚Äôt always on our minds**

In the planning stage, we considered having multiple applications for different segments of the population ‚Äì one for seniors, one for working adults, one for parents. 

This approach had several advantages ‚Äì individual apps are faster to develop, and simpler to design ‚Äì but we soon came to realise a citizen often wears many hats. 

For example: A user can be a father, a vehicle owner, working a 9-5 job, as well as a volunteer on weekends.

As of May 2019, there were some 170 affiliated government apps available ‚Äì an impressive array of services, no doubt ‚Äì but once you take into consideration the differences in learning curve, hassle of using multiple apps and the cost of maintaining these apps, the cons really started to add up.

### **That‚Äôs why we created LifeSG to make life easier**

The thinking behind LifeSG is simple: the less time citizens spend transacting with the government, the more time they get to focus on the things they love.  

To make that a reality, we‚Äôve created one app with one user interface, one simplified user experience, across all multiple government services ‚Äì learning to use the app will be easy and actually using it will be even easier.

### **1. Relevance through Curation**

Citizens are busy people with limited time and attention. They are also an incredibly diverse group of people, each with different needs.
