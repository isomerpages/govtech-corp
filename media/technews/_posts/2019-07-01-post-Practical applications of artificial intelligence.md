<h3>Fit-for-purpose AI</h3>
<p>Contrary to popular sentiment, AI is not a homogenous technology nor a
magic bullet for all problems. Rather, Dr Enzell noted that AI is an umbrella
term encompassing innovations in the fields of natural language processing,
machine translation, image and graph analytics, visualisation, and much
more. Hence, matching the appropriate technological capability with the
right use case is critical.</p>
<p>In China, Mr Lu noted how AI and sensors had great potential to transform
municipal services, such as waste disposal. Unlike in the past, where the
removal of rubbish in trash bins was carried out at fixed intervals, sensors
can now send an alert to the relevant agency when a trash bin is nearing
maximum capacity. At the same time, AI is applied to analyse data on disposal
patterns for predictive waste clearance. ‚ÄúBy doing this, we can better
manage the hygiene level of an area,‚Äù said Mr Lu, adding that a similar
approach can be taken with predictive maintenance of elevators.</p>
<h3>Deciding with data</h3>
<p>Both panellists agreed that the foundation for developing practical AI
applications lies in the ability to collect and integrate data. Dr Enzell
highlighted the need for standards for data classification and rules or
guidelines surrounding data security.</p>
<p>Visualisation of data, once it has been analysed by AI, is just as important,
said Mr Lu. He showed how the command centre of the Jing‚Äôan district in
Shanghai, China, has an integrated interface that displays event assessment

geospatial map, giving authorities a bird‚Äôs-eye view of city management
while allowing them to zoom in on specific details as and when it is necessary.</p>
<p>Given the promise of AI-assisted decision making, some delegates in the
audience wondered if there was a better way to measure the impact that
AI systems are having when applied to digital governance. Dr Enzell mentioned
that measuring the impact of each individual AI use case is a helpful first
step. for example, one might register a reduction in the time taken to
complete a transaction online after the implementation of an AI solution.</p>
<p>Clearly, finding practical use cases for AI is just the beginning, and
more in-depth research and discussion are needed to fully understand how
to realise the full potential of AI while avoiding its perils.</p>
